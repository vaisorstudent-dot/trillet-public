01
Two Agents, Two Proof Jobs

Start with two, not five. Each MVP has a distinct job. The homepage concierge is a router-plus-prefill instrument: it reduces friction, reflects the visitor's business back to them, and hands the next page structured context. The solutions voice demo is a proof-of-quality instrument: it produces one vivid belief update -- "this sounds deployable."

Both are built on the same template primitives agencies will use. If the site agents require custom one-off logic, we prove the product in marketing while failing to create a reusable asset.

Agent Job Format Phase
Homepage Concierge Qualify, route, prefill downstream Text, opt-in, embedded in hero MVP
Solutions Voice Demo Prove voice quality in 30 seconds Live voice call from browser MVP
Enterprise Intake Qualify enterprise, capture scope Text, dark-mode styled Phase 2
Pricing Advisor Recommend tier, calculate margin Text Phase 3
Platform Expert Handle competitive questions Text Phase 3

Additionally, the homepage includes an economics calculator (flat form, no AI, instant math) with an optional "Voice to discuss with the Economic Advisor" escalation button. The form captures data even from visitors who never engage with voice.

03
Solutions Voice Demo

The centerpiece. This is where the visitor hears the product. Not a recording, not a simulation -- a live voice call from the browser. The emotional target: "I would trust this to represent me when I'm not available."

Two-level selection before the call: pick a vertical, then pick a scenario. This is not open-ended -- the demo is scenario-bounded. Tightly controlled experiences outperform open sandboxes because they can be made exceptional rather than merely adequate.

VerticalScenarios
DentalNew patient booking · Insurance question · After-hours emergency triage
LegalNew case intake · Consult scheduling · Existing client status
Real EstateBuyer inquiry · Listing inquiry · After-hours lead capture
Home ServicesEmergency service call · Quote request · Schedule reschedule
Pre-Call Framing

Before the call starts, the visitor sees: "Hear how a Trillet agent would answer a real [vertical] call. This is a live demo based on a common [scenario]. Takes about 30 seconds."

This sets expectations, reduces anxiety about open-ended conversation, and frames the experience as proof, not test.

Caller I'd like to book a first appointment, a cleaning.

Agent Wednesday afternoon works. I have a 2:30 with Dr. Patel. I'll put you down and text you a confirmation with the office address and parking info. Anything else I can help with?

The first 3 seconds matter most. The opening should sound like someone who has already oriented to the caller's likely need -- poised, warm, lightly anticipatory. Not an IVR menu. Not a generic "how can I help you." A greeting that signals: I know what people usually call about, and I'm ready.

Recovery is the real differentiator. When something goes wrong, a premium agent reduces effort for the caller: "No problem -- the simplest way is for me to ask two quick questions and point you the right way." Never repeats menu options mechanically. Never blames the caller. If recovery fails, exits gracefully: "I'm going to hand this back to the demo page so you can try another scenario."

Post-call reflection: "That was a sample of how Trillet handles [scenario] for [vertical] businesses -- greeting callers, collecting key details, and moving them to the next action." Then a CTA framed as continuation of service: "See how this would be configured for your business."

Exceptional

No audible hesitation. Correct terminology. One moment where the visitor thinks "that sounded like a real front desk."

Adequate

Understandable and on-topic. Completes the scenario. No errors.

Anti-marketing

Latency >4 seconds. Wrong terminology. Awkward barge-in. Fails to capture the scenario premise.

04
Template Architecture: Three Layers

The site agents are not one-off builds. They are the first deployments of a template system. The template has three layers: invariants that Trillet enforces, bounded policies that agencies can tune within safe ranges, and content that agencies fully own.

Agencies parameterize what the agent says. Trillet controls how it says it.

The premium feel is not polish on top of mechanics.
It is the highest-value part of what gets cloned.
Layer What Control
Invariant No pressure. Exit grace. Reduce effort under confusion. No mechanical repetition. Continuity of attention. Service before extraction. Permission sensitivity. Non-negotiable. Trillet enforces. Agencies cannot override.
Bounded Message length (1-4 sentences). Choice presentation (1-3 options). Pacing. Generosity pattern. Bonus elements. Restraint level. Strong defaults. Tunable within safe ranges. Can't break the quality floor.
Content Brand. Vertical. Domain knowledge. Scenarios. Opening lines. Routes. Tone profiles. Intake fields. Fully agency-owned. This is their creative space.

The concierge is a single template with parameterized paths. The voice demo is a template family -- one per vertical, with scenario-level parameterization. Both share the same invariant layer.

Why constraints? Agencies who want full control and are willing to build bad experiences can use raw APIs. Trillet's value to agencies IS the quality floor: "Your agents sound premium because our templates enforce it." The guardrails are the feature.

06
Questions for Ming

The strategic question from the Flagship Deployment thesis was: can we build the winning interactions so they become repeatable deployment primitives?

The implementation questions that follow from it:

Template Architecture

1. Can the concierge and voice demo be built as actual templates on the existing engine -- same primitives agencies would deploy?

2. Can service invariants (no pressure, exit grace, service-before-extraction) be enforced at the engine level so agencies inherit them automatically?

3. Can bounded policies (message length, pacing, choice limits) be exposed as configurable-within-range rather than binary on/off?

Voice Quality

4. Can we hit first-audio <2 seconds and first-turn <3 seconds consistently? What infrastructure changes are needed?

5. Can scenario-bounded demos be built so the agent stays on-script without feeling robotic? What's the prompt engineering approach?

6. What's the voice quality ceiling with current ElevenLabs / voice stack? Is the "holy shit" moment achievable today?

Cross-Agent & Data

7. Can session state pass between the concierge and the economics calculator (and later, between pages)?

8. How do conversation data and lead signals reach the sales team -- CRM integration, webhook, or manual?

9. What's realistically achievable before China? Concierge prototype? Voice demo prototype? Template schema design?

The site agents are not marketing widgets.

They are the first deployments of a template system where the premium interaction architecture is infrastructure, not polish.

If we build them right, every agency that deploys a Trillet template inherits the quality standard automatically.
