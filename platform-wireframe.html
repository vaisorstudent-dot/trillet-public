Why full-stack matters

Most voice AI platforms are wrappers. This is the engine.

Competitors assemble voice agents from third-party STT, TTS, LLM, and telephony providers. When something breaks, no one owns the problem. Trillet controls the full stack -- from speech recognition to call routing to agent logic -- so there's one team accountable for every call.

What "full-stack" actually means

Three things that change when one team owns the whole path.

One failure surface
When a call drops, latency spikes, or an agent gives wrong information, there's one team to call. Not a chain of vendors pointing at each other. Wrapper platforms can't debug across layers they don't own.
Optimized end-to-end
Voice latency compounds across every layer. When STT, LLM, TTS, and telephony are separate vendors with separate APIs, each handoff adds 50-200ms. Full-stack means one optimization path from speech input to speech output.
No dependency ceiling
Wrapper platforms inherit their vendors' limits. When Retell changes pricing, every Retell wrapper reprices. When Twilio has an outage, every Twilio wrapper goes down. Owning the stack means your growth isn't capped by someone else's roadmap.

The wrapper problem

What happens when your platform is someone else's API.

This is not theoretical. These are real failure modes agencies on wrapper platforms deal with.

Upstream outage
Twilio goes down for 45 minutes. Every agent on every wrapper platform using Twilio goes silent. Your clients' phones stop being answered. Your SLA is someone else's SLA.
Surprise repricing
Retell raises per-minute pricing by 30%. You find out on a blog post. Your margins collapse overnight on contracts you've already signed with clients. Renegotiate or absorb.
Debug dead end
Call quality drops. Is it STT latency? LLM response time? TTS rendering? Telephony jitter? When you don't own the layers, you can't see across them. You submit tickets to three vendors and wait.
Feature ceiling
Client needs custom call routing logic. Your platform vendor says "that's not on the roadmap." You wait, work around it, or migrate. Your product roadmap is someone else's backlog.
Latency stacking
STT API: 150ms. LLM API: 400ms. TTS API: 200ms. Telephony roundtrip: 100ms. Total: 850ms before the agent speaks. Each vendor is "within spec." The caller hears dead air.
Vendor lock-in
You've built 200 agents on a wrapper platform. That platform's provider changes terms or shuts down. Your agents, your clients' configurations, your brand -- all hostage to a dependency you chose once.
Common questions

What comparison shoppers ask us.

"Do you actually own the speech layer or do you use Deepgram/ElevenLabs?"
Trillet operates its own speech pipeline optimized for telephony-grade audio. We're not reselling Deepgram or ElevenLabs -- the speech-to-text and text-to-speech processing runs on our infrastructure.
"What happens if Twilio has an outage?"
Trillet maintains direct carrier relationships alongside Twilio as a fallback path. Most wrapper platforms are Twilio-only -- when Twilio goes down, they go down. Our telephony layer has redundancy built in.
"Can I deploy on-prem or in a private cloud?"
Yes. Trillet is one of the only voice AI platforms that supports full on-premise deployment inside client infrastructure. This matters for healthcare, financial services, and government clients with strict data residency requirements.
"Why is there no concurrency limit?"
Concurrency limits exist because wrapper platforms pay per-slot to their upstream provider. Since Trillet owns the voice pipeline and telephony, there's no per-slot cost to pass through. Your clients don't get busy signals during peak hours.
"What's the actual latency?"
Sub-500ms first-token response time in production, including STT + LLM + TTS. Wrapper platforms typically land 700-1200ms because each vendor handoff adds latency that can't be optimized across boundaries.
"Is the white-label actually full, or do you restrict it on lower tiers?"
Full white-label on every plan. Your domain, your logo, your login page, your client-facing emails. No "powered by" badges. No feature-gating the brand. Entry, Core, and Scale all get the same white-label depth.
