How engagements work

Every deployment starts with understanding the environment.

Enterprise work is not sold from a product page. It begins with a conversation about your operational reality and ends with a deployment that matches it.

Step 1
Discovery and operating fit
Map the environment: call volume, office topology, existing systems, compliance requirements, and what the deployment actually needs to handle in practice.
Step 2
Scoped proposal
Define the implementation: required integrations, phasing plan, support model, and commercial structure. All agreed before work begins.
Step 3
Implementation and launch
Phased deployment with closer coordination and explicit ownership. Typically starts with one site or workflow, validates, then expands.
Step 4
Ongoing managed operations
The relationship continues through managed support, change requests, optimization, and deeper deployment expansion as the use case proves out.
Who this is for

When the cost of getting it wrong is higher than the cost of doing it right.

The enterprise path exists for organizations where reliability, control, and deployment confidence matter more than fast self-serve onboarding.

The workflow is high-stakes
Financial services, healthcare, government, collections. Environments where a dropped call, a wrong answer, or a compliance gap is not a minor inconvenience. You need a deployment partner who understands that.
You need more than platform access
You need integration into existing systems, phased rollout across locations, training and handoff to internal teams, and someone accountable when something needs to change. Software credentials and a help center are not enough.
The environment has real constraints
On-prem requirements. PCI or HIPAA compliance. Multi-site deployments with different call flows per location. Internal approval processes that need a proper proposal, not a pricing page.

Common questions

What enterprise buyers ask first.

"Is this the same product as the agency plans?"
Same core platform. Different engagement model. Enterprise gets scoped implementation, deeper integration, managed operations, and a commercial structure built around your environment — not a self-serve plan.
"What's the minimum engagement?"
Scope, timeline, and commercial structure are defined in discovery before any commitment. Every deployment is different — we'll tell you exactly what it costs after understanding your environment.
"How long does implementation take?"
Typical phased deployments run 4–8 weeks from scoped proposal to first-site launch. Multi-site expansions are staged based on validation results from the initial deployment.
"Can we deploy on-premise?"
Yes. The full stack — speech recognition, language model, telephony, agent logic — runs inside your infrastructure. No cloud dependency, no data leaving your network. This is not a proxy or partial deployment.
"What compliance standards do you support?"
PCI environments are supported today. HIPAA and SOC2 certification are in progress. On-prem deployment inherits your existing compliance infrastructure, which is the strongest posture available.
"What happens after launch?"
The managed operating path includes ongoing support, change requests, optimization, and expansion planning. Enterprise relationships do not end at deployment — they evolve with your use case.
